## Top 3 New App Ideas
1. Messenger App
   - Mobile: A messaging app uses mobile-specific features, such as camera, audio, push notifications, and real-time updates.
   - Story: The story is compelling because it offers clear value to the audience by connecting them. Friends and peers would likely respond well to it, as messaging is inherently social.
   - Market: The market for a messaging app has a large size and scale of potential users, almost everyone need a chat app to communicate with friends.
   - Habit: This app idea is highly habit-forming. The average user would likely open the app frequently to check messages. The users will invite more people to chat with them on this app if they are used to it.
   - Scope: A messenger app is appropriately technically challenging. Even a stripped-down version (e.g., just sending basic text without images) is still interesting to build and clearly defined.
2. Health Fitness Tracker
   - Mobile: This idea is highly mobile. It can easily incorporate two or more mobile features like location (GPS for running), sensors (accelerometer for steps), and push notifications (workout reminders).
   - Story: The app provides a clear value to the audience by helping them track their health and fitness goals.
   - Market: The market has a large potential user base, but it can also be tailored to provide huge value to a niche group (e.g., powerlifters or marathon runners). The audience is well defined.
   - Habit: A fitness tracker relies heavily on being habit-forming. If designed well, the average user will open the app frequently to log workouts or check progress.
   - Scope: The scope is well-formed and the product is clearly defined. A stripped-down version, such as a basic step counter or simple workout logger, would still be an interesting project to build.
4. Subscription and Expense Tracker
   - Mobile: To make it uniquely mobile, this app could incorporate features like the camera (to scan receipts) and push notifications (to alert users of upcoming subscription renewals).
   - Story: It offers clear value to the audience by helping users save money and manage their finances.
   - Market: This idea has a large scale of potential users, as many people look to manage their personal finances.
   - Habit: Financial apps can be habit-forming if users are encouraged to log expenses daily and once they are used to record their expense everyday, they will frequently use this app.
   - Scope: The product is clearly defined. The stripped-down version like only a logger for expense and subscription is still interesting to build because that's the core function and is needed most.
